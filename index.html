Key Insights

Ontario and Quebec consistently account for over 55% of total Canadian employment throughout the dataset.
Alberta saw the sharpest growth rate, more than doubling employment between 1976 and 2019, driven largely by the energy sector.
The gender gap has narrowed significantly. Female employment grew faster than male employment in almost every province since 1976.
Full-time employment in Ontario dropped sharply in 1982 and 1991, clearly showing the impact of recessions on the labour market.
Prince Edward Island is the smallest workforce but showed consistent steady growth with no major dips unlike larger provinces.
